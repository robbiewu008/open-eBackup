Logging In to DeviceManager

You can log in to DeviceManager from the OceanProtect WebUI to configure, manage, and maintain the system.

Precautions

Use different types of browsers to access the OceanProtect and DeviceManager management pages. Otherwise, when a user logs out of or switches the language on one management page, the other management page exits or the language is switched at the same time.

Applicable to OceanProtect X Series Backup Appliances

  • Method 1:
    1. Choose System > Infrastructure > Cluster Management.
    2. On the Backup Clusters tab page, click a node name under the Local Cluster Nodes area.
    3. On the displayed Node Details page, click Open the device management platform to go to DeviceManager.
  • Method 2:
    1. On the maintenance terminal, open a browser.
    2. Enter https://XXX.XXX.XXX.XXX:8088 in the address box and press Enter. The DeviceManager login page is displayed.

      XXX.XXX.XXX.XXX indicates the management network port IP address of the OceanProtect.

    3. On the DeviceManager login page, enter the username and password and click Log In to display the DeviceManager management page.

      Contact the system administrator to obtain the username and password used for logging in to DeviceManager.

Applicable to OceanProtect E1000 (with the OceanProtect Used as Backup Storage)

If the OceanProtect is used as backup storage, perform the following steps to log in to DeviceManager of the backup storage:
  1. On the maintenance terminal, open a browser.
  2. Enter https://XXX.XXX.XXX.XXX:8088 in the address box and press Enter. The DeviceManager login page is displayed.

    XXX.XXX.XXX.XXX indicates the management network port IP address of the backup storage device.

  3. On the DeviceManager login page, enter the username and password and click Log In to display the DeviceManager management page.

    Contact the system administrator to obtain the username and password used for logging in to DeviceManager.
