Managing Fileset Protection

This section describes how to modify, remove, activate, or disable fileset protection on the OceanProtect.

Related Operations

Log in to the management page, choose Protection > Big Data > HDFS, click the Filesets tab, and locate the desired file set.

Table 1 describes the related operations.

Table 1 Operations related to fileset protection

Operation

Description

Navigation Path

Modifying protection

Scenario

This operation enables you to modify a protection plan to associate another SLA with a fileset and modify the execution scripts before and after fileset protection. After the modification, the next backup will be executed based on the new protection plan.

Note

The modification does not affect the protection job that is being executed. The modified protection plan will take effect in the next protection period.

In the row where the fileset resides, choose More > Modify Protection.

Removing protection

Scenario

This operation enables you to remove the protection of a fileset when it does not need to be protected. After the removal, the system cannot execute protection jobs for the fileset. To protect the fileset again, associate an SLA with it again.

Note

Protection cannot be removed when a protection job of the fileset is running.

In the row where the fileset resides, choose More > Remove Protection.

Activating protection

Scenario

This operation enables you to re-activate a disabled protection plan of a fileset. After the protection plan is activated, the system backs up the fileset based on the protection plan.

In the row where the fileset resides, choose More > Activate Protection.

Disabling protection

Scenario

This operation enables you to disable the protection plan of a fileset when its periodic protection is not needed. After the protection plan is disabled, the system does not execute the automatic backup job of the fileset.

Note

Disabling protection does not affect ongoing backup jobs.

In the row where the fileset resides, choose More > Disable Protection.

Manual backup

Scenario

This operation enables you to perform a backup immediately.

Note

Copies generated by manual backup are retained for the duration defined in the SLA.

In the row where the fileset resides, choose More > Manual Backup.

Modifying a fileset

Scenario

This operation enables you to modify the fileset name, and the selected directory and table in a fileset.

In the row where the fileset resides, choose More > Modify.

Deleting a fileset

Scenario

This operation enables you to delete a fileset if you do not need to protect it.

Note

A fileset that has been associated with an SLA cannot be deleted.

In the row where the fileset resides, choose More > Delete.

Note: The procedure for restoring HDFS filesets is similar to that in Restoration and is not described here.
