Managing Exchange Single-Node Systems or Availability Groups

You can add, remove, activate, or disable protection for Exchange single-node systems or availability groups on the OceanProtect. You can also perform operations such as manual backup, resource scanning, modification, and deletion.

You need to log in to the management page, choose Protection > Applications > Exchange, click the Single-Node Systems/Availability Groups tab, and locate the target single-node system or availability group for management.

Table 1 describes the related operations.

Table 1 Operations related to Exchange single-node systems/availability groups

Operation

Description

Navigation Path

Modifying protection

Scenario

You can modify a protection plan to associate another SLA with a single-node system/availability group or modify a user-defined script. After the modification, the next backup will be executed based on the new protection plan.

Note

  • The modification does not affect the protection job that is being executed. The new protection plan will take effect in the next protection period.
  • If the SLA associated with a resource changes, you are advised to modify the current SLA on the Protection > SLAs page. You are advised not to associate the resource with another SLA by modifying protection.

    When you associate the resource with another SLA by modifying protection, the license capacity may be deducted repeatedly if the copy of the original SLA still exists.

Choose More > Modify Protection in the row where the single-node system/availability group resides.

Removing protection

Scenario

You can remove the protection plan if a single-node system/availability group no longer needs to be protected. After the removal, the system cannot execute protection jobs for the single-node system/availability group.

Note

  • Protection cannot be removed when a protection job of the single-node system/availability group is running.
  • If the SLA associated with a resource changes, you are advised to modify the current SLA on the Protection > SLAs page. You are advised not to associate the resource with another SLA after removing protection.

    When you associate the resource with another SLA after removing protection, the license capacity may be deducted repeatedly if the copy of the original SLA still exists.

Choose More > Remove Protection in the row where the single-node system/availability group resides.

Activating protection

Scenario

This operation enables you to activate a disabled protection plan for a single-node system/availability group. After the protection plan is activated, the system backs up the single-node system/availability group based on the protection plan.

Note

You can perform this operation only when the single-node system/availability group is in the Inactivated state.

Choose More > Activate Protection in the row where the single-node system/availability group resides.

Disabling protection

Scenario

This operation enables you to disable the protection plan of a single-node system/availability group when its periodic protection is not needed. After the protection plan is disabled, the system does not execute the automatic backup job of the single-node system/availability group.

Note

Disabling protection does not affect ongoing backup jobs.

Choose More > Disable Protection in the row where the single-node system/availability group resides.

Performing manual backup

Scenario

This operation enables you to perform a backup immediately.

Note

Copies generated by manual backup are retained for the duration defined in the SLA.

Choose More > Manual Backup in the row where the single-node system/availability group resides.

Scanning resources

Scenario

You can scan databases or mailboxes below a registered single-node system/availability group and display them on the Databases or Mailboxes tab page as objects to be protected.

Note

No scanning result is displayed when a single-node system/availability group is offline or you do not have the permission.

Choose More > Resource Scan in the row where the single-node system/availability group resides.

Testing connectivity

Scenario

This operation enables you to test the connectivity between the OceanProtect and resources to be protected.

Choose More > Test Connectivity in the row where the single-node system/availability group resides.

Modifying a single-node system/availability group

Scenario

You can refer to this section to modify the name and host node information of a single-node system or availability group.

Choose More > Modify in the row where the single-node system/availability group resides.

Deleting a single-node system/availability group

Scenario

If you no longer need to protect databases of a single-node system or availability group, you can delete the single-node system or availability group. After the deletion, related information cannot be viewed on the OceanProtect.

Note

If a database or mailbox below a single-node system or availability group has been associated with an SLA, the single-node system or availability group cannot be deleted.

Choose More > Delete in the row where the single-node system/availability group resides.

Adding a tag

Scenario

This operation enables you to quickly filter and manage resources.

Note
  • The system administrator can add a maximum of 10,000 tags to a resource.
  • A non-system administrator must have the permission of Production resource management to add tags to resources.
  • A non-system administrator can add a maximum of five tags to a resource.
Choose More > Add Tag.
NOTE:
  • On the displayed Add Tag dialog box, you can select existing tags or click Create to create a tag.
  • To modify or delete a tag, click Go to Tag Management on the Add Tag page to go to the Tag Management page.

Removing a tag

Scenario

This operation enables you to remove the tag of a resource when it is no longer needed.

Choose More > Remove Tag.

Note: The procedure for restoring Exchange resources is the same as that described in Recovery.
